Origin: e-Estonia pioneers
Nortal built much of Estonia's legendary e-government infrastructure — e-tax, e-census, digital identity. This is foundational to their identity. They are not a generic IT shop — they believe technology can fundamentally reshape how governments and societies work.
🤝
100% employee-owned
Since 2016, Nortal has no external investors — owned entirely by its people. This is rare and matters culturally: decisions are made by engineers and leaders who have skin in the game. Long-term thinking beats short-term quarterly pressure.
🇱🇹
Lithuania is strategic, not just a satellite
Vilnius is one of Nortal's key delivery centres — 80% hands-on engineers working on Fortune 100 clients in the US, Germany, Finland, Sweden. Lithuania is described as a "strategic division," not a support hub. Your work will be visible globally.
🤖
AI Lab in Lithuania — right now
Nortal is launching an AI Lab in Lithuania, funded by the €50M bond issued in 2024. They're actively building AI-powered delivery and enterprise AI solutions. This is directly relevant to your self-hosted LLM and MCP infrastructure work.
🚀
2024–2025: Aggressive expansion phase
Nortal acquired 3DOT Solutions (UK cybersecurity/defence), Nearsure (650+ LatAm engineers, now rebranded as Nortal), and Questers (Bulgaria). They issued €50M in bonds. Appointed a former Royal Navy Rear Admiral as UK CEO. This is a company in rapid, ambitious growth — not a stable maintenance company. They need people who can build at scale, not just operate.
🛡️
Defence & cybersecurity push
Major 2025 strategic focus. Hired senior defence industry veterans. Acquired 3DOT Solutions. UK CEO is a former Royal Navy Rear Admiral. If you have any compliance/regulated environment experience, tie it here.
🌍
Core sectors: public sector & enterprise
Healthcare, government, fintech, energy, manufacturing, defence. All regulated, all high-stakes. This maps perfectly to your background in fintech and government platforms. These clients demand the same rigor you've been operating under.
Culture & values
Pillar 01
Commit to delivering value
Not just shipping code — delivering outcomes that matter to clients and society. They reject projects they can't win. Quality over volume.
Pillar 02
Take ownership and initiative
They want engineers who don't wait to be told. Self-starters who see a problem and fix it. This is the core trait they hire for at senior level.
Pillar 03
Empower yourself and others
Invest in your own growth and enable those around you. Mentoring, knowledge sharing, team leadership — all expected, not optional.
Pillar 04
Own your future and growth
Personal development plans, global mentorship, unlimited access to top education platforms. They expect you to drive your own career, not wait for a manager to plan it.
🎯
Key hiring traits (their words)
Creativity, ambition, responsibility for final value. Persistent problem solving. Active communication that converts technology into business value. High bar for quality. Not "we need bodies" — "we accept projects that have tech-positive impact on society."

Questions to ask Nortal (shows real interest)
AI Lab What's the current state of the Lithuania AI Lab — what are you building first, and where does infrastructure ownership sit? ▼
"I've read about the AI Lab you're launching here in Vilnius. What's the current focus — are you building internal tooling, client-facing AI products, or both? And where does the infrastructure ownership sit — within the lab team, or shared platform?"
Shows you did your research. Signals you're thinking about where you'd fit operationally, not just conceptually.
Strategy How does Nortal differentiate between a good client engagement and a great one — and how does the platform/DevOps function contribute? ▼
"Nortal has a policy of only taking on projects you're confident you can deliver. In practice, what separates a project that goes into the case study from one that just meets the contract? And how does the infrastructure and platform layer contribute to that differentiation?"
Shows you understand the consulting model and are thinking about how to be exceptional, not just competent.
Lithuania How does the Lithuania delivery centre engage with global clients — is it autonomous or a satellite? ▼
"I know Lithuania handles Fortune 100 client work across the US, Germany, Finland. In practice, how autonomous is the Vilnius team in technical decision-making — do you own architecture decisions locally, or are you executing direction from other offices?"
Tests whether this is a real engineering role or staff augmentation. Important for understanding how much ownership you'll actually have.
Culture What does the engineering leadership structure look like — how are technical decisions made on a client engagement? ▼
"You're employee-owned, which suggests a relatively flat decision-making culture. On a typical platform or DevOps engagement, who has the final call on architectural decisions — the lead engineer, an internal CTO function, or is it driven by the client?"
Reveals the real power structure. At senior level, you need to know if your judgment will be trusted or overridden.
Growth What would success look like for this role 12 months in? ▼
"If I joined and we were having this conversation 12 months from now — what would you want to be able to say I'd contributed? What problem would ideally be solved that isn't solved today?"
Classic but powerful. Shows you're results-oriented and want to deliver, not just settle in. Also gives you intelligence on what they actually need.
